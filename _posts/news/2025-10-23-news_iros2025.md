<p>
Members of the HARCO Lab participated in the <strong>IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS)</strong>, held in <strong>Hangzhou, China</strong>.
</p>

<p>
During the conference, <a href="/members/seungmin_choi">Seungmin Choi</a> gave a presentation titled:<br>
<em>"Stable Variable Impedance Control Via CLF-MPC for Physical Human-Robot Interaction"</em><br>
</p>

<p>
In addition, <a href="/members/wansoo_kim">Prof. Wansoo Kim</a> delivered an invited talk at the workshop:<br>
<em>"Human-Centric Exoskeletons and Collaborative Robots in the Age of Industry 5.0: Advancing Safe, Adaptive, and Intuitive Interaction"</em><br>
This presentation was part of the “Action and Interaction: Humans and Robots in Collaboration” (Half Day Workshop)
highlighting human-aware shared autonomy frameworks for safer and more intuitive human–humanoid coexistence.
